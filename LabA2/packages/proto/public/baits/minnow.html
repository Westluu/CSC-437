Minnows

The bluntnose minnow is a primary bait fish for Northern America

About

Minnow is the common name for a number of species of small freshwater fish, belonging to several genera of the family Cyprinidae and in particular the subfamily Leuciscinae. They are also known in Ireland as pinkeens.[1] While the common name can refer to a range of taxa, smaller fish in the subfamily Leusciscinae are considered by anglers to be "true" minnows.[2]

As Food

While primarily used for bait, minnows can also be eaten directly by humans. Some Native American cultures have used minnows as food.[8] If minnows are small enough, they can be eaten whole.[9]
