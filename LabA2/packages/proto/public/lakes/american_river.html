American River

The American River is a ~120-mile-long river in California, United States. It originates in the Sierra Nevada and flows westward through the Sacramento Valley, eventually merging into the Sacramento River in Sacramento. The river is known for its gold rush history, recreational activities, and wildlife.

Interesting Facts

  • The American River is a key part of California's Gold Rush history, with the discovery of gold at Sutter's Mill in 1848.
  • It is a popular location for recreational activities, including rafting, fishing, and hiking.
  • The river is home to an abundance of wildlife, including salmon runs, which are critical for the local ecosystem.
