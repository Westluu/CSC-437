Yangtze River

The Yangtze River is the longest river in Asia and the third-longest in the world. It flows through China from the Tibetan Plateau to the East China Sea. The river is essential for China's economy and culture.

Interesting Facts
  • The Yangtze River is approximately 3,915 miles long.
  • The Three Gorges Dam on the Yangtze is the world's largest power station in terms of installed capacity.
  • The river basin is home to over 400 million people, one-third of China's population.
