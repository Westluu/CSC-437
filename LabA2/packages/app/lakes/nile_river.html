Nile River

The Nile River is the longest river in the world, flowing northward through northeastern Africa to drain into the Mediterranean Sea. It has been a vital lifeline for civilization in Egypt since ancient times.

Interesting Facts

  • The Nile River is approximately 4,135 miles long.
  • The river's annual floods were crucial for agriculture, depositing nutrient-rich silt on the land.
  • The Nile has two major tributaries: the White Nile and the Blue Nile, which meet near the Sudanese capital of Khartoum.
