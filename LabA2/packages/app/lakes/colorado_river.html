Colorado River

The Colorado River is a major river in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its dramatic canyons and whitewater rapids. It runs through the Grand Canyon and is a vital water source for the arid regions it traverses.

Interesting Facts

  • The Colorado River is approximately 1,450 miles long, starting in the Rocky Mountains of Colorado and ending in the Gulf of California.
  • The river has carved out the Grand Canyon over millions of years.
  • The Colorado River provides water to over 40 million people and irrigates nearly 5.5 million acres of farmland.
