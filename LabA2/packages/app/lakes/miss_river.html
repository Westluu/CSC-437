Mississippi River

The Mississippi River is the second-longest river in North America, flowing from its source at Lake Itasca in Minnesota to the Gulf of Mexico. It is a critical waterway for transportation, commerce, and ecology.

Interesting Facts

  • The Mississippi River is approximately 2,340 miles long.
  • It serves as a major transportation route, with barges carrying goods up and down the river.
  • The river's basin covers over 1.2 million square miles, including parts of 31 U.S. states and two Canadian provinces.
