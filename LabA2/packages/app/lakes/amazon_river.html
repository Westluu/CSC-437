Amazon River

The Amazon River is the largest river by discharge volume of water in the world and the second-longest river. It flows through South America and is surrounded by the vast Amazon Rainforest, which is home to a diverse range of wildlife.

Interesting Facts

  • The Amazon River is approximately 4,000 miles long.
  • The river's discharge is greater than the next seven largest independent rivers combined.
  • The Amazon Rainforest, through which the river flows, is often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth" because of its crucial role in absorbing carbon dioxide and producing oxygen.
